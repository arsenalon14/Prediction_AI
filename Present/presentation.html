Demand Censoring & Tobit MLE

When stock sells out, sales drop to zero. The remaining customer demand is hidden, or "censored".

  • Latent Demand Recovery: Standard forecasting models under-predict because they fail to see lost sales.
  • Tobit MLE Fitting: Maximum Likelihood Estimation models uncensored sales using Normal PDF, and stockouts using Survival Functions.
  • Statistical Parameters: Recovers true mean demand rate (μ) and standard deviation (σ) for each calendar month.

Optimization Math

Step-by-Step Q* Calculation Pipeline

Our engine executes a four-step pipeline to compute the optimal load Q* for each flight.

  • Step 1: Tobit Rate Estimation (T2): Fits Tobit model on historical rates per passenger r = units_sold / net_bob per month, yielding true μ_rate and σ_rate.
  • Step 2: Margin Service Level (T3/T4): Solves Critical Fractile F* = (Price - Cost) / Price to find optimal z-score buffer.
  • Step 3: Baseline Scaling (T5): Multiplies rates by upcoming Net BoB: Q*_base = (μ_rate * net_bob) + (z * σ_rate * net_bob).
  • Step 4: Demographic Mix Scale (T5b): Applies Gamma factor γ for the upcoming manifest: Q*_final = ceil(γ * Q*_base).
The Math Pipeline
Rate parameters: μ_rate, σ_rate (Monthly Tobit MLE)
Critical Fractile: z = Φ-1((p - c)/p)
Flight Scale: Q* = ceil( γ × net_bob × (μ_rate + z × σ_rate) )

Operations Research

The 7-Theory Operations Research Pipeline

Our optimization engine is built on 7 academic and industry operations research theories:

  • T1: Pair-Route Aggregation — Sums outbound and return legs to eliminate cabin crew logging error.
  • T2: Demand Censoring (Tobit) — Maximum Likelihood model to find latent demand during stockouts.
  • T3: Newsvendor Model — Formulates underage cost (lost sales margin) vs overage cost (wastage).
  • T4: Critical Fractile — Establishes targeted service level based on product financial margins.
  • T5: Flight-Specific Scaling — Scales baseline demand rate to the upcoming flight passenger capacity.
  • T5b: Demographic Gamma (A1) — Adjusts loads based on upcoming manifest nationality deviation.
  • T6: Product Substitution (Cold Start) — Matches new menu items to historical proxy SKUs.
  • T7: MLE Refit (Feedback Loop) — Automatically updates parameters using post-flight actuals uploads.

Data Quality

Clean Data: Water Code Correction

Eliminating denominator mismatch errors is critical to forecasting integrity.

  • The Bug: Live manifests counted water bottles as perishable PBMs, whereas training data excluded them.
  • The Impact: Artificially lowered the net boarded BoB count (Nactive), causing a systematic 8–11% under-catering on every flight.
  • The Solution: Automated filters exclude non-perishable water codes (BWFD, BWAK, BWHL, WBHL) in all live calculations.
