The Thai AirAsia In-Flight Perishable Sales Story

Perishable inventory in budget airlines operates under a strict, unforgiving condition: meals are loaded once at the main hub (**DMK**) and must be discarded at the end of the return flight loop (**MLE-DMK**) to maintain food safety and avoid outstation handling fees. Our comprehensive data audit of passenger manifests, PBM pre-bookings, and in-flight transactions for the years 2024, 2025, and early 2026 has unlocked three ground-breaking strategic insights that will serve as the core engines of our AI forecasting model:

1. Outbound-Only Logging (Pair-Route Proof)

Our audit reveals **100% of TAA's wastage logs are recorded under the outbound sector (Flight 175).** The aircraft physically operates the round trip as a single catering cart session. This confirms our **S1+S2 Collapsed Pair-Route** approach is the exact, operationally correct modeling unit.

2. 2026 Demographic Shift & PBM Surge

Historically, Thais pre-booked heavily while Chinese tourists relied on BoB, causing unpredicted demand surges. Early 2026 data reveals a massive shift: YoY passenger volumes surged (Mar +55.9%), and Maldivian travelers emerged as a dominant force, driving a **+160.8% explosion in PBM bookings** in Jan 2026.

3. Under-catering vs. Spoilage Waste

In 2024, the airline suffered from severe under-catering (February stockouts hit **57.3%**). In 2025, reactive over-loading caused severe food waste (2,262 units wasted in July). With 2026's shifting demographic mix, the AI model will use these changing probabilities to calculate the exact mathematical sweet spot.

Passenger Demographic Key Insights

Demographic characteristics and seasonal waves

A. Summer Chinese Peak

During July and August, overall traffic reaches its maximum, driven by Chinese family tourists. They represent 41.0% to 53.9% of all boarded passengers. Kids under 12 reach their yearly high of 13.1% to 14.1%.

B. April Thai Songkran Peak

April is highly influenced by Thai holiday travel, representing the #1 passenger segment at 43.6% (2024) and 47.6% (2025), driving high fresh food demand outbound.

C. Maldivian Winter Segment

In November, traffic dips to low season, and Maldivian utility passengers traveling to Bangkok rise to their highest share (21% to 25% of all passengers).

Catering Dilemma

2024: Systemic Under-Catering

Low loading levels caused severe stockouts on hot meals. **Nasi Lemak ran out on 50.9% of all flight pairings**, Chicken Teriyaki at **43.3%**, and Boba Tea at **37.2%**. February stockout rate hit **57.3%**.

2025: Massive Over-Provisioning Spoilage

To prevent 2024 stockouts, the airline loaded massive stocks in peak months (e.g., loading 3,645 perishables in July 2025). This reduced stockouts to **16.1%**, but caused a food waste spike (**2,262 wasted units in July alone**).

Thai Travelers: The Core Anchors

Account for over **50%** of all PBM bookings. In-flight, their favorite hot meal is spicier comfort food: **ML NOI Fried Chicken with Basil on Rice**.

Chinese Travelers: The Cart Surger

Almost never pre-book meals, relying 100% on the cart. Their top favorite meal is the sweet **Chicken Teriyaki** (2024) and **Palmyra Somtam Chicken** (2025).
